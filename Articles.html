Featured Deep Dive

Why do we sleep?

It sounds fundamental, but for the past eight years, sleep science has undergone a radical shift. We know it helps with memory consolidation and "brain washing" (the glymphatic system), but the core evolutionary reason why animals must become unconscious and vulnerable remains a primary mystery in biology.

Every year, thousands of students ask online: "If evolution is about survival, why did we develop a state that makes us completely defenseless for 8 hours a day?"

The latest theories suggest it might be a required metabolic reset that cannot happen while the brain is processing external stimuli. But as of 2026, we still don't have a definitive "Unified Theory of Sleep."
