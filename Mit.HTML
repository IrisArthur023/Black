Popular Questions

Science Ages 8–14
"Why do we only ever see one side of the Moon?"
♥ 4,821 found this helpful Read full answer →
The Moon is tidally locked to Earth — it rotates on its axis at exactly the same rate it orbits us. This synchronous rotation means the same hemisphere always faces our planet. It took billions of years of gravitational interaction to reach this state, and the same phenomenon happens with many moons throughout the solar system.
Math Ages 10–13
"Why do we need algebra if we have calculators?"
Algebra teaches logical thinking and pattern recognition — skills that apply far beyond arithmetic. Calculators solve specific numbers; algebra helps you solve unknown situations.

History Ages 12+
"How did the internet get invented?"
It began as ARPANET, a US military research project in 1969. Scientists needed to share data between universities. Over decades it evolved from a handful of connected computers into the global network we have today.
